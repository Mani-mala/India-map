Nagaland - The Land of Festivals

Geography

Nagaland is a northeastern state of India, bordered by Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, and Myanmar. It is known for its **hilly terrain, dense forests, and vibrant tribal culture**.

Culture

Nagaland is home to **16 major tribes**, each with its own unique traditions and customs. The state is famous for the **Hornbill Festival**, traditional warrior dances, and handicrafts.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Kohima
  • Largest City: Dimapur
  • Official Language: English
  • Famous Festivals: Hornbill Festival, Moatsu Festival
  • Major Rivers: Doyang, Dikhu
  • Chief Minister: Neiphiu Rio

Tourist Attractions

Popular places to visit include **Dzukou Valley, Kohima War Cemetery, Mon Village, Japfu Peak, and the scenic Mokokchung district**.

Assam - The Land of Tea Gardens

Geography

Assam is a state in northeastern India, bordered by Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram, Tripura, Meghalaya, and West Bengal, as well as Bangladesh and Bhutan. The **Brahmaputra River** flows through the state, making it one of the most fertile regions in India.

Culture

Assam has a rich cultural heritage, known for **Bihu dance, Assamese silk (Muga silk)**, and traditional Sattriya dance. The state celebrates vibrant festivals like **Bihu and Ambubachi Mela**.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Dispur
  • Largest City: Guwahati
  • Official Language: Assamese
  • Famous Temples: Kamakhya Temple, Umananda Temple
  • Popular Festivals: Bihu, Ambubachi Mela
  • Major Rivers: Brahmaputra, Barak
  • Chief Minister: Himanta Biswa Sarma

Tourist Attractions

Popular places to visit include **Kaziranga National Park** (home to the one-horned rhinoceros), **Majuli Island**, **Manas National Park**, and the scenic tea estates of Assam.

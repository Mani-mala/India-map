Sikkim - The Land of Mountains

Geography

Sikkim is a small state in northeastern India, bordered by Tibet, Bhutan, Nepal, and West Bengal. It is home to the world's third-highest peak, **Mount Kanchenjunga**, and is known for its lush green valleys, rivers, and diverse flora and fauna.

Culture

Sikkim has a rich cultural heritage influenced by Tibetan Buddhism. The state is known for its monasteries, vibrant festivals, and traditional music and dance forms.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Gangtok
  • Largest City: Gangtok
  • Official Languages: Nepali, Sikkimese, Lepcha, English
  • Famous Monasteries: Rumtek Monastery, Pemayangtse Monastery
  • Popular Festivals: Losar, Saga Dawa
  • Major Rivers: Teesta, Rangeet
  • Chief Minister: Prem Singh Tamang

Tourist Attractions

Popular tourist destinations include Tsomgo Lake, Nathula Pass, Yumthang Valley, Pelling, and the breathtaking Kanchenjunga National Park.

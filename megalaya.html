Meghalaya - The Abode of Clouds

Geography

Meghalaya is a northeastern state of India, bordered by Assam and Bangladesh. It is known for its lush green hills, heavy rainfall, and beautiful waterfalls. The state is home to **Cherrapunji and Mawsynram**, two of the wettest places on Earth.

Culture

Meghalaya is rich in tribal culture, mainly dominated by the Khasi, Garo, and Jaintia tribes. The state is known for its **traditional music, dance forms, and festivals like Shad Suk Mynsiem and Wangala Festival**.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Shillong
  • Largest City: Shillong
  • Official Language: English
  • Famous Festivals: Nongkrem Dance, Wangala Festival
  • Major Rivers: Umngot, Simsang
  • Chief Minister: Conrad Sangma

Tourist Attractions

Popular places to visit include **Cherrapunji, Mawsynram, Umiam Lake, Dawki (Umngot River), and the Living Root Bridges of Meghalaya**.

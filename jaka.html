Jammu & Kashmir - Paradise on Earth

Geography

Jammu & Kashmir is located in the northernmost part of India, bordered by Pakistan, China, and the Indian states of Himachal Pradesh and Punjab. It is known for its scenic beauty, including valleys, lakes, and mountain ranges, particularly the famous Dal Lake and the Himalayas.

Culture

Jammu & Kashmir is rich in cultural heritage, with influences from Persian, Mughal, and Central Asian cultures. The region is known for its traditional handicrafts, including Pashmina shawls and Kashmiri carpets. Festivals like Eid, Diwali, and Baisakhi are widely celebrated.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Srinagar (summer), Jammu (winter)
  • Largest City: Srinagar
  • Official Language: Urdu
  • Famous Temples: Vaishno Devi Temple, Amarnath Cave
  • Popular Festivals: Eid, Diwali, Baisakhi
  • Major Rivers: Jhelum
  • Chief Minister: Lieutenant Governor Girish Chandra Murmu

Tourist Attractions

Popular tourist destinations in Jammu & Kashmir include Srinagar, Gulmarg, Pahalgam, Leh, and the Vaishno Devi Temple. The state is famous for its houseboats, trekking trails, and the vibrant Shikara rides on Dal Lake.

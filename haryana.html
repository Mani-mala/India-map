Haryana - The Land of Milk and Agriculture

Geography

Haryana is located in the northern part of India, bordered by Punjab, Himachal Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, and Delhi. It is known for its agricultural contributions and dairy industry.

Culture

Haryana is famous for its folk dances like Ghoomar and Phag, vibrant festivals, and wrestling tradition. Hindi is the official language, with Haryanvi being widely spoken.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Chandigarh (shared with Punjab)
  • Largest City: Faridabad
  • Official Language: Hindi
  • Famous Temples: Mata Mansa Devi Temple, Sheetla Mata Temple
  • Popular Festivals: Teej, Lohri
  • Major Rivers: Yamuna, Ghaggar
  • Chief Minister: Nayab Singh Saini

Tourist Attractions

Some famous tourist places include Kurukshetra, Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary, Pinjore Gardens, and Surajkund.
